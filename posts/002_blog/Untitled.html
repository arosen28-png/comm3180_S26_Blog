Spring Break in Philly: Worth Staying or Not?

Every Spring Break, people in Philly ask the same question:
should I travel somewhere warm, or can I just stay here and hope for good weather?

Philly likes to act like spring starts in March, but sometimes it still feels like winter. Other days feel surprisingly nice. I wanted to see what Spring Break weather in Philly is actually like, not just what it feels like in one random year.

To do this, I looked at daily weather data from Philadelphia from 2005 to 2025 and focused on the usual Spring Break window in mid-March.

What Spring Break Usually Feels Like

When people think of Spring Break, they imagine warm days and being outside. In Philly, that sometimes happens — but not always.

Looking at the data, most Spring Break days fall somewhere in the middle. They’re usually not freezing, but they’re also not consistently warm. Some days reach the low or mid-60s, while others stay much cooler.

Basically, Spring Break in Philly is unpredictable. You might get a really nice day that feels like spring, and then the next day feels like winter again.

Rain Is the Real Problem

Cold weather can usually be handled with a jacket, but rain is harder to deal with. Rain ruins plans and makes it harder to enjoy being outside.

The data shows that rain happens pretty often during Spring Break. Most years have at least one or two rainy days during that week. Snow is much less common, but it still happens occasionally, which is kind of shocking for March.

This means that even if the temperature isn’t terrible, the weather can still mess up your plans.

Why March Weather Is So Annoying

One of the biggest problems with Spring Break weather in Philly is how much it changes during the day.

Many days have a large gap between the high and low temperatures. That means it might feel okay in the afternoon, but be really cold in the morning or at night.

March weather doesn’t stay consistent, which makes it hard to plan outfits or activities. It’s the kind of weather where you leave the house dressed for one season and come back dressed for another.

So… Should You Stay in Philly for Spring Break?

Based on the data, staying in Philly for Spring Break is a gamble.

You will probably get at least a few decent days. You’ll also probably deal with rain, cold mornings, or sudden weather changes. Snow isn’t common, but it’s not impossible either.

If you’re okay with unpredictable weather and want to save money, staying in Philly isn’t a terrible choice. But if you want guaranteed warmth and sunshine, Philly in March might not be the best bet.

Spring Break in Philly isn’t amazing — but it’s not awful either. It’s just unpredictable.
